📚 How Video Review Works

▼
🎯 Quick Summary: CritterCatcherAI uses a two-stage pipeline (YOLO → CLIP) to detect and identify animals. Your job is to review videos and provide training data to improve CLIP profile accuracy.

Stage 1: YOLO Detection (Automatic)

YOLO is a fast AI that detects 80 broad categories (person, dog, bird, car, etc.). Videos are automatically sorted into folders like /data/review/dog/ or /data/review/unknown/ based on what YOLO detected.

  • Fast but not precise - YOLO can tell "dog" from "cat" but not "Jack Russell" from "Beagle"
  • Unknown category - Videos where YOLO couldn't identify anything, or confidence was too low
  • Your current category tab shows which YOLO category you're reviewing

Stage 2: CLIP Profiles (Your Training Data)

CLIP is a slower, more precise AI that you train with examples. It can identify specific species or objects (e.g., "Hedgehog" vs "dog", or "Finch" vs "sparrow").

  • Positive examples - Videos/frames that CONTAIN the target animal ("Yes, this IS a hedgehog")
  • Negative examples - Videos/frames that DO NOT contain the target ("No, this is NOT a hedgehog")
  • Both are required - CLIP learns by comparing what something IS vs what it ISN'T
⚠️ Important: Provide at least 10 positive AND 10 negative examples before CLIP training will work effectively. The more examples you provide, the better CLIP becomes at identifying your target animals.

Review Workflow Options

Quick Actions (Simple Batch Operations):

  • ✓ Confirm Videos - Move to /data/sorted/{category}/, no training data extracted
  • ✗ Reject Videos - Delete videos (optionally save as negative examples for a profile)
  • → Assign to Profile - Extract frames as positive OR negative (all videos get same treatment)

🎯 Advanced Review (Per-Video Control):

  • Configure each video individually (positive, negative, both, or skip)
  • Assign different videos to different profiles in one operation
  • Mix actions: some positive, some negative, some delete
  • Use this for "unknown" videos where you need to manually identify and sort

What Happens to Videos

  • Positive training → Frames saved to /data/training/{profile}/confirmed/ + video copied to /data/sorted/{profile}/
  • Negative training → Frames saved to /data/training/{profile}/rejected/, video deleted
  • Both → Frames saved to both folders, video copied to sorted
  • Confirm only → Video moved to sorted, no training frames extracted
  • Reject/Delete → Video removed from review folder
💡 Pro Tip: When reviewing "unknown" videos:
  1. Select all videos that are clearly NOT your target animal → Assign as negative examples
  2. Select all videos that clearly ARE your target animal → Assign as positive examples
  3. For mixed/uncertain batches, use Advanced Review to configure each video individually
